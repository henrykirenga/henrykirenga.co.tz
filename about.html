About Me

Hi, I'm Henry Kirenga, a visual artist born in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania.

My Artistic Vision

My work is rooted in freedom. I don't confine myself to a single subject or style I paint portraits, animals, nature, emotions, and anything that sparks inspiration. Whether it's a quiet expression on someone's face or the wild energy of a charging bull, every painting is a chance to explore new ideas and connect with people on a deeper level.

"Art is my language of freedom each stroke breaks boundaries, each color tells a story untold by words."

I mainly use acrylic paint, working with both brushes and palette knives, with the knife being my favorite tool for building rich texture and movement. I'm especially inspired by my environment from the earthy textures of coffee seeds, to the colorful patterns of African fabrics, and the natural beauty that surrounds me in Tanzania. These elements help form a unique visual language in my work.

My Artistic Journey

My journey as an artist has been shaped by curiosity, culture, and a need to express what words can't. Over time, I've developed a style that allows me to shift freely between subjects not because I'm undecided, but because I believe art should be as open and expressive as life itself.

Musical Collaborations

Worked with Nias Nyalada Art Studio during special painting sessions with leading Tanzanian musicians, including Diamond Platnumz's family and Jux.

Art for All

I choose to keep my art affordable and accessible, especially for my local community. It's not because I value it less but because I believe that art should be for everyone, not just a few. For international collectors, that means supporting work that's rooted in purpose and integrity. For locals, it means making space for connection through creativity.
